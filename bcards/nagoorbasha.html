Services Offered

Endoscopy

An endoscopy is used in medicine to look inside the body. Endoscopy procedure uses an endoscope to examine the interior of a hollow organ or cavity of the body. Unlike many other medical imaging techniques , endoscopes are inserted directly into the organ.

Colonoscopy

The visual inspection of the large intestine (colon) using an illuminated, adjustable video endoscope is a colonoscopy. The colon starts in the lower right abdomen, and as it passes up and down the abdomen, it appears like a large question mark, culminating in the rectum. Its length is 5 to 6 feet. The colon has a variety of functions, including the removal of water from the liquid stool that enters it for the development of a shaped stool.

LIver Problems

Your liver does a lot of things that keep you healthy. It turns nutrients into chemicals your body needs. It filters out poisons. It helps turn food into energy. So when your liver doesn’t work well, that can affect your whole body. Different things can cause serious liver conditions. You’ll want to know about the top causes.

Endoscopic Ultra Sound

Endoscopic ultrasound (EUS) is a minimally invasive procedure to assess digestive (gastrointestinal) and lung diseases. A special endoscope uses high-frequency sound waves to produce detailed images of the lining and walls of your digestive tract and chest, nearby organs such as the pancreas and liver, and lymph nodes.

Gastric Banding

Gastric banding is a surgical treatment for obesity. These kinds of intervention are known as bariatric surgery. The procedure constricts the stomach, so that a person feels full after eating less food than usual. It is approved for use as a weight loss treatment by the Food and Drug Administration (FDA).

Polypectomy

A polypectomy is a procedure used to remove polyps from the inside of the colon, also called the large intestine. A polyp is an abnormal collection of tissue. The procedure is relatively noninvasive and is usually carried out at the same time as a colonoscopy. A colonoscopy is first done to detect the presence of any polyps. If any are detected, a polypectomy is performed and the tissue is removed. The tissue will be examined to determine if the growths are cancerous, precancerous, or benign. This can prevent colon cancer.
